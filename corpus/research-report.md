# Packing Ground Truth v0 — Indian Students Moving to the US

*Synthesized 2026-07-13 from a fan-out deep-research sweep (17 sources: 6 university international-student-office pages, 8 packing blogs/consultancies, 2 Quora threads, 1 price-reference site). Reddit blocked the crawler, so community dissent is drawn from comment-driven aggregator blogs (notably HappySchools) that echo the same r/f1visa consensus.*

> **Confidence note.** The research workflow was interrupted by an org spend limit during its adversarial-verification phase. Only the two UIUC climate/documents claims were fully 3-vote verified (`high`). Everything else is *extracted-but-unverified* — real quotes from real pages, but not independently cross-checked — and is marked `medium` (consistent primary/multi-source) or `low` (single blog/forum). Treat this as a strong first draft to correct with real user feedback, not gospel.

## The decision rule the sources keep circling

One Quora answer stated the underlying heuristic cleanly: **needs are cheaper in India, wants are cheaper in the US.** Food, everyday clothing, tailoring, medicines, and eyewear are far cheaper at home and worth the luggage weight. Branded goods, electronics, and appliances are cheaper (and voltage-correct) in the US. Overlay two hard constraints — **US customs** (no perishables/seeds/raw agri) and **~23 kg × 2 checked bags** — and almost every verdict falls out.

## Strong consensus (build these into the default checklist)

- **Documents — bring, carry-on.** Passport, I-20, SEVIS receipt, financial proofs, transcripts, immunization records, plus photocopies and scans. Never in checked baggage. *(UIUC, verified; Clark; Wesleyan)*
- **Prescription medicines — bring 1-2 months + doctor's letter.** US prices are multiples of India's; some states reject foreign/out-of-state prescriptions, so carry a signed description with generic names. *(Yocket, IDP, upGrad, Elmhurst, Hamilton)*
- **Eyeglasses/contacts — bring 1-2 spare pairs.** ~$225/pair in the US even with insurance vs a fraction in India. *(HappySchools; corroborated by a first-hand price blog)*
- **Bedding — buy/ship in the US.** US dorm beds are Twin XL (39"×80"); Indian sheets won't fit, and linens are bulky. Cheap on arrival (sheets $15-25, blanket $20-75). This is the most unanimous *buy* verdict across universities. *(Wesleyan, Hamilton, Miami OH, Elmhurst)*
- **Indian 220V appliances — don't bring.** US is 110-120V; they need heavy transformers or simply fail. Bring only chargers + Type A/B adapters. *(Hamilton, Elmhurst, Wesleyan, Quora)*
- **Perishable/homemade food, seeds, produce — skip (hard rule).** CBP/USDA confiscates them; undeclared items risk penalties. *(UIUC verified, Avanse, Miami)*
- **Toiletries, stationery, textbooks — buy in the US.** Bring a ~1-month starter kit; everything else is cheap locally and universities run orientation shopping trips. Don't pack heavy books. *(Hamilton, HappySchools, Clark, IDP, upGrad)*
- **Consumer electronics — bring your working laptop/phone, buy new in the US.** Significantly cheaper (iPhone 11 Pro $999 vs ₹99,900); Black Friday is the moment. *(Quora, WeMakeScholars, Numbeo)*

## Contested items (flag both sides in the UI — this is where the app earns trust)

**Winter jacket** — the flagship disagreement. Universities in cold zones split: Wesleyan and Miami OH say *bring* winter gear (expensive in the US); Elmhurst (Illinois) and most blogs say *buy the heavy coat on arrival* — better for sustained sub-zero cold and cheap on sale (down jacket ~$100). The reconciling consensus: **bring thermals + gloves + a light/transit jacket; buy the heavy down coat in the US.** Fall arrivals have weeks to buy before winter; Spring arrivals need a warm jacket for day one. Bring a full heavy coat only if you already own a good one.

**Pressure cooker** — blogs say *bring* (central to Indian cooking, pricier in the US); forums and community comments say *buy/skip* (heavy, red-flagged and hand-inspected at security, transport cost ≈ US price, Instant Pot $60-80 on sale, seniors will lend one). Resolution turns on **housing and location**: dorm dwellers skip it; apartment cooks near an Indian community should buy an Instant Pot locally; only those headed somewhere remote should bring one.

**Rice and lentils** — the sharpest split, but customs breaks the tie toward **skip**: US rules restrict raw rice and unpackaged lentils (Khapra beetle), they're the worst weight-to-value item you can pack, and they're available (if ~7x pricier) at US Indian stores. A minority of blogs still recommend a small sealed bridge supply.

**Spices** — mostly *bring* (dry, sealed, declared — cheaper at home, marked up ~4x in the US), with a lone caution (IDP) about plant products. Keep quantities reasonable and everything sealed.

## Context dimensions that flip verdicts (feed these into the profile builder)

| Dimension | What it changes |
|---|---|
| **Climate zone** (cold/Midwest/Northeast vs warm/South/West) | Whether winter gear matters at all; how much |
| **Intake** (Fall vs Spring) | Fall = buy coat after arrival; Spring = land into winter, bring one |
| **Housing** (dorm vs apartment) | Dorm = Twin XL bedding, little kitchen; apartment = buy furnishings/kitchen locally |
| **Diet** (heavy home-cooking vs eats out) | Priority of pressure cooker, tava, spices |
| **Proximity to an Indian grocery / community** | The hidden variable behind rice, spices, and cooker verdicts |
